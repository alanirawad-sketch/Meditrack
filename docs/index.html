Target Audience

  • The "Quantified Self" Engineer: Users who already automate their lives via scripts and want to pipe health data into their custom dashboards.
  • Biohackers & Protocol Adherents: Individuals managing complex supplement stacks (Nootropics, Longevity protocols) who value precision and speed.
  • Privacy Purists: Users in sensitive professions who refuse to store medical data on third-party servers.
  • Remote Workers: People who spend 8+ hours in a terminal and need a non-intrusive "nudge" to stay on top of their wellness.

Monetization Strategy

The "Open-Core" Approach

  • Free: Unlimited local tracking, JSON exports, and core CLI functionality.
  • Pro ($5/mo):
    • End-to-end encrypted cloud sync.
    • Beautifully templated PDF/LaTeX reports for medical professionals.
    • SMS/Desktop notification hooks.

Growth & User Attraction

We aren't just building a tool; we're building a workflow.

  • Developer Ecosystem: Releasing a meditrack-api crate to allow other Rust developers to build plugins or TUI (Terminal UI) wrappers.
  • Technical Content: Publishing "Why I switched from MyFitnessPal to the CLI" on Hacker News and Dev.to to spark the "minimalism" debate.
  • The "Rice" Factor: Encouraging users on r/UnixPorn to share their terminal setups featuring Meditrack's sleek output.
  • Package Manager Presence: Getting Meditrack into Homebrew, AUR, and Scoop to reduce friction to a single command.
